Performance Data Recorder

Record, share and analyze your driving experiences on and off the track with the industry-exclusive Performance Data Recorder1. Add an extra dimension to your drive by logging video of your drives, along with real-time performance data, on an SD memory card stored in the glove box. Four modes — Track, Sport, Touring and Performance — allow you to capture video, audio, driving stats, date and time, and a lot more.

Intelligent interior design

Stingray’s interior space is pure and dynamic, with a single sweeping contour unifying instrument console and panel, and door trim. The smaller, 360mm super-tight steering wheel gives you a more direct, race-track feel.

This system also houses the 20GB hard drive and hosts the ConnectedDrive Services which includes BMW Apps, BMW Emergency Call, BMW Teleservices, BMW Online Services, Information Plus and Remote Services.

Of course all of the above is controlled via the firm’s iDrive interface, and the cabin is cooled and heated (remotely if you wish) by a dual-zone climate control system. On the move you’ll notice the colour head up display, though be aware that with some polarised sunglasses the information becomes invisible, the dynamic cruise control, variable dampers, front and rear parking sensors, automatically dimming rear view mirror and a rain sensor for the lights and wipers.

Those lights are adaptive LED items, and compliment the layered glass exterior design and standard 20-inch alloy wheels, black brake calipers, and blue accent detailing.

Every owner can take advantage of the BMW Apps Interface, which allows owners to monitor the state of the car’s charge, whether the lights, windows, boot or doors are open or on as well as browse previous trip information remotely from their smartphone. A remote control function allows the car to be pre-heated or cooled before setting off, as well as searching and locating charging points before starting the journey

BMW i8 Extra Equipment

When it comes to options there’s not much choice for the i8, but the easiest way to make your example look different is to change the paint from Sophisto Grey (standard) to Ionic Silver or Protonic Blue (both £450) or Crystal White (£1,350). All wheels are 20-inches, but there are two different styles to choose from (W-Spoke at £1,150 and Turbine-Spoke at £1,380).

Blue seat belts will set you back £310, an anthracite headlining £295, while the upgraded Harman/Kardon stereo adds another £895 to the list price. Choose the Driving Assistant with Surround-view camera and High-beam assistant for the lights for £1,490 while the eDrive exterior sound option (so pedestrians can hear you coming when in electric mode) will set you back £85.

There’s the option to change the interior trim as well, with the Carpo Interior World scheme (£1,150) or Halo Interior World (£2,150), the latter adding a leather that has been tanned with Olive leaf extract for those with an eco-conscience.

From November 2014 the BMW i8 will be offered with the option of Laserlights, which double the high-beam range of traditional LED headlights but consume 1/3 less energy. The BMW 360 degree Electric Products and Services includes Service Inclusive, BMW i Mobile care, and the option to add a specially designed BMW i Wallbox for your home.

A Pure Impulse Design Package takes the standard interior scheme and lifts it one step further with blue accents, Amido metallic trim and details in Pearl Gloss and Chrome, as well as an Anthracite headlining. It costs over £10,000 but also allows you to fully immerse yourself in the exclusive BMW i dream, should you want to, allowing membership to the Pure Impulse Programme inviting owners to eat in the fanciest restaurants, stay at the swankiest hotels and experience money-can’t buy events.
